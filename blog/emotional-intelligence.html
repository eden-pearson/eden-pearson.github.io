Emotional Intelligence

What is emotional intelligence?

Emotional intelligence is the ability to recognize, understand and manage your own emotions as well as recognize, understand and influence the emotions of people around you.

There are five key elements to emotional intelligence:

  1. Self-awareness - The ability to recognize and understand ones own emotions, abilities and values.
  2. Self-regulation - The ability to manage and regulate ones own emotions and reactions
  3. Motivation - being self-motivated and having a strong internal drive to achieve goals and persevere during challenges
  4. Empathy - The ability to perceive and interpret others' emotions
  5. Social skills - manage relationships using communication, active listening, conflict resolution and teamwork

How is EQ different to IQ:

IQ measures a persons cognitive abilities and intellectual potential. It focuses on skills such as problem-solving, analytical skills, memory and logical reasoning. IQ determines how well an individual can understand and process information.

EQ measures how well an individual can understand and manage emotions. It focuses on the skills outlined above: self-awareness, self-regulation, empathy, motivation and social skills. EQ determines how well someone can navigate social situations, communicate effectively and exhibit empathy towards others.

Why is EQ important

Q plays a significant role in professional success, leadership effectiveness, quality of relationships, mental health, and overall well-being. By developing our emotional intelligence, we can cultivate more fulfilling relationships, excel in our careers, and lead more balanced and satisfying lives.

Some research to back this up:

  • TalentSmart found that EQ accounts for 58% of job performance across all job types, surpassing other factors like IQ and technical skills.
  • The University of California found individuals with higher emotional intelligence had greater overall life satisfaction and reported higher levels of happiness and well-being.
  • A survey by CareerBuilder found that 71% of employers value emotional intelligence over IQ when hiring and promoting employees.
  • The Consortium for Research on Emotional Intelligence in Organizations found that EQ training programs resulted in improved employee performance, enhanced leadership effectiveness, stronger teamwork, and reduced turnover rates.
  • A study published in the Journal of Applied Psychology found that leaders with higher EQ exhibited better leadership effectiveness, including higher job performance ratings and greater employee satisfaction.
