Te Whare Tapa Whā

Te Whare Tapa Whā is a holistic model of wellbeing developed by Māori health advocate Tā Mason Durie. It encompasses four dimensions of well-being: taha wairua (spiritual well-being), taha hinengaro (mental and emotional well-being), taha tinana (physical well-being), and taha whānau (family and social well-being) and also the connection to whenua (land).
