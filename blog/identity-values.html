My identity and values

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

A co-worker of mine was in charge of creating a marketing campaign for an HIV medication. As they presented their campaign strategy to me, they made quite a few comments and assumptions about their target audience that were homophobic. I then had to decide whether or not to confront this person about their behaviour. Part of me did not want to say anything as this co-worker was considerably more senior than me, and I didn't want to ruin our working relationship. After weighing my options, I decided that the right thing to do would be to use open communication and confront this co-worker to let them know their actions were wrong. Fortunately, this co-worker took my feedback on board and re-evaluated part of their marketing campaign. Upon reflection, I am glad I chose to confront my co-worker, and I think we are both better off for it.

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity

In my family, education is highly valued, which helped push me towards getting a University degree. I also come from a very loving and supportive family, which has led me to be loving and supportive of others. While at university, I have made many intelligent and hard-working friends, which inspires me to do my best to emulate those qualities in myself. In today's day and age, we are also largely influenced by the people we surround ourselves with on social media. Hence, I aim to follow people with qualities I wish to embrace myself.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

My strengths include being logical, persistent, and a good team player. I also have a passion for learning which will come in handy during Dev Academy as opportunities will be abundant for me to learn new things.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

My limitations include being introverted, a slow decision-maker, and risk-averse. Being introverted means I'll want to withhold from participating in class discussions and am less likely to ask questions when needed.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why

Last year I was tasked with organizing a fundraising event with a small group of my colleagues. We all had different ideas for which direction to take for our event and couldn't agree on which one was best, which caused tensions to arise. At first, we started leaning towards the idea of the loudest person in the group as they were the most insistent with their concept. However, I decided we should all have an equal chance to share our ideas. We did this, and then we took a vote to determine which was the best option. Using democracy was effective and caused us to agree on a single direction to take our event eventually. In future, I would take a more collaborative approach and combine aspects of multiple people's ideas rather than focusing on a single person's idea.
