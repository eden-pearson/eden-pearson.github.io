Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

A co-worker of mine was in charge of creating a marketing campaign for an HIV medication. As they were presenting their campaign strategy to me, they made quite a few comments and assumptions about their target audience that were homophobic. I then had to decide whether or not to confront this person about their behaviour. Part of me did not want to say anything as this co-worker was considerably more senior than me, and I didn't want to ruin our working relationship. After weighing up my options I decided that the right thing to do would be to use open communication and confront this co-worker to let them know that their actions were wrong. Fortunately, this co-worker took my feedback on board and re-evaluated their marketing campaign. Upon reflection, I am glad I chose to confront my co-worker, and I think we are both better off for it.

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity

In my family education is highly valued, which helped push me towards getting a University degree. I also come from a very loving and supportive family which has led me to be loving and supportive of others. While at university I made many friends who are all very intelligent and hard-working people, which has inspired me to do my best to emulate those qualities in myself. In todays day and age I think that we are also largely influenced by the people that we surround ourself with on social media. Hence, I aim to follow people that exhibit qualities that I wish to embrace myself.

I also have a lack of self confidence so I’m less likely to volunteer my answer to questions.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why

Last year I was tasked with organizing a fundraising event at work with a small group of my colleagues. We all had different ideas for which direction to take for our event and couldn't agree on which one was best which caused tensions to arise. At first we started leaning towards the idea of the loudest person in the group as they were the most insistent with their idea. However, I decided that we should all get an equal chance to share our ideas. We did this and then we took a vote to decide which was the best option. This was effective and caused us to eventually agree on a single direction to take our event. In future I would take a more collaborative approach and combine aspects of multiple peoples ideas rather than focusing on a single persons idea.
