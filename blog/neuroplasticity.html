Neuroplasticity and growth mindset

Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity is the ability of the brain change and form new neural connections especially in response to experiences. Understanding the principles of neuroplasticity is empowering as it lets us embrace the idea that our abilities are not fixed but can be improved through deliberate practice and perseverance.

I plan on engaging with the principles of neuroplasticity by embracing challenges and practicing deliberate practice. I will also remind myself about the principles of neuroplasticity when I am struggling.

We can increase our neuroplasticity by learning a new skill such as a new language, playing an instrument, painting or drawing. Additionally practicing meditation, physical exercise and engaging in social interactions can increase our neuroplasticity.

Growth Mindset

Growth mindset is a term coined by psychologist Carol Dweck which refers to the belief that our abilities and intelligence can be developed through dedication, hard work, and resilience. It contrasts with a fixed mindset, which assumes that our qualities and capabilities are fixed and unchangeable. People who have a growth mindset are more likely to embrace challenges, have a higher persistence and see failures as opportunities for growth.

I used to view success as getting the right answer and proving my innate intelligence. After learning about growth mindset, I am going to start viewing success as anytime that I experience personal growth regardless of the overall outcome.

I will integrate a growth mindset into my learning journey by actively seeking out and embracing challenges and seeing them as an opportunity for growth. I will value effort and perseverance instead of solely seeking successful outcomes. I will also actively seek constructive feedback from facilitators and peers to use as a tool for personal growth and development.

Learn more about growth mindset

Reflection

With neuroplasticity in mind, I am going to approach my learning plan with a focus on deliberate practice and continuous improvement. I am also going to keep up my habits of physical exercise and practicing mindfulness. I will incorporate a growth mindset by embracing a positive attitude toward challenges and failures. Instead of being discouraged by setbacks, I will see them as opportunities for learning and improvement.
