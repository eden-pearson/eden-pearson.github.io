Foundations Reflection

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

  1. The importance of subduing my ego to allow for authentic self-expression. I have learned to recognize when my ego hinders my ability to communicate well.
  2. Through the core learning experience, I have gained confidence in my ability to analyze complex issues and find innovative solutions.
  3. Perhaps most surprising to me is the revelation that I possess the capability to grasp intricate coding concepts.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

In the realm of programming, values, empathy, and self-awareness play vital roles. Firstly, having empathy towards oneself is crucial when learning to code, considering the inherently challenging and gradual nature of the process. Additionally, empathy towards others is essential as programming often involves collaborative teamwork. Self-awareness is a fundamental aspect of growth as a programmer. By reflecting on our strengths, weaknesses, and biases, we can identify areas for improvement. Self-awareness empowers us to leverage our strengths effectively, address weaknesses, and mitigate biases that may hinder our problem-solving abilities. Values provide a moral compass for our programming endeavours. They shape our problem-solving approaches and guide our software development practices. Upholding strong values ensures ethical and responsible coding practices, fostering a positive impact in the digital realm.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

The most astounding aspect of my core learning journey has been the unexpected enjoyment I derived from it. Initially skeptical, I entered this experience with the belief that it could potentially be a futile use of time. However, to my surprise, I discovered immense satisfaction and fulfillment in the process.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

Engaging in self-analysis presented a considerable challenge throughout the core learning process. Assessing my own strengths and weaknesses proved to be a daunting task, requiring introspection and critical evaluation. Moreover, establishing a daily practice of mindfulness meditation has been challenging to incorporate into my routine.

Why do you think Dev Academy, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Dev Academy prioritizes core learning within their web development Bootcamp course because they recognize that being proficient in coding alone does not make a candidate truly employable. In order to produce well-rounded individuals who can thrive in a professional environment, it is essential to foster qualities beyond technical skills. Skills such as effective teamwork, problem-solving, adaptability, and embracing challenges are nurtured through core learning, making graduates more versatile and valuable to potential employers.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practice programming instead?

Absolutely not. I firmly believe that the time dedicated to studying core learning has been far from wasteful. In fact, it has significantly enhanced my programming abilities. Core learning has equipped me with valuable problem-solving techniques, enabling me to approach challenges with a systematic plan rather than relying solely on brute force. Moreover, learning about neuroplasticity and embracing a growth mindset has revolutionized my perception of obstacles. Instead of succumbing to overwhelm and giving up, I now confront challenges head-on, persevering through them. This shift in mindset has not only transformed my coding skills but also cultivated resilience and determination, qualities that transcend the realm of programming and positively impact all aspects of my life.
